-- 4b. LIMITATIONS  (~200 words) --

Write about at least 4 limitations in your own words:
  1. Single dataset: this model was trained and tested on one Kaggle dataset.
     It may not generalise to MRI scans from different hospitals, scanners,
     or protocols. Write why this matters for clinical deployment.
  2. 2D slices only: MRI is naturally 3D volumetric data. Using 2D slices
     loses spatial context between consecutive slices.
  3. Interpretability partially addressed: Grad-CAM heatmaps were generated to
     visualise model attention (Section 7, part2d). However, this was applied to
     a small number of samples only. Full clinical interpretability would require
     systematic validation across hundreds of cases, and clinician review of whether
     highlighted regions match actual tumour locations on radiology reports.
  4. MRI scan type not controlled: the dataset mixes different acquisition
     types (T1, T2, FLAIR). A model trained on mixed types may be less
     accurate on a specific type.
  5. Meningioma still hardest class even with class weights — write about this.

-- 4c. FUTURE WORK  (~150 words) --

Write 4-5 suggestions in your own words:
  1. Systematic Grad-CAM validation: Grad-CAM was demonstrated on select samples
     in this work. Future work should apply it to all misclassified cases and have
     clinicians verify whether heatmap regions correspond to actual tumour locations
  2. Ensemble models: combine predictions from CNN and EfficientNetB0
     to reduce individual model errors
  3. 3D volumetric CNN: process the full MRI volume rather than 2D slices
  4. Multi-hospital dataset: train and test on data from multiple scanners
     to improve generalisability
  5. Clinical validation: evaluate the model alongside radiologist decisions
     to measure actual clinical utility
